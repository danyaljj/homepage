  1. Bunded integral operator: I am given the following equation $int_{y=a}^{b} f(x,y) X(y) dy = b(x)$, where f(x,y) and b(x) are known.

  • What conditions do I need in order to have a unique solution for $X(y)$?

  • How can I find $X(x)$? (Even as special case can be so useful).
    Importance: Note that this is a interesting generalization to linear matrix multiplication. One could get important results by solving this problem. Generalize a lot of notions in linear algebra using this new notion of continuous matrix(Psedu-inverse is of special interest!).
